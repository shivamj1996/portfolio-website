HCI E-Commerce Website

HTML/CSS/PHP/JS

For my final year project I chose to research how HCI principles could be implemented into E-Commerce websites in order for them to gain a better user experience(UX). After finding the different techniques developers use to get a better UX, I then transferred my knowledge into an actual product: a small-scale E-Commerce fashion website.


Firstly I conducted a literature review in order to give myself an overview of the topic, looking at in-depth case studies, research in the field of HCI, results collected by the likes of the Nielsen Norman Group, and conference papers relevant to HCI and E-Commerce.

Then I conducted an expert walkthrough for 4 online fashion retailers: Zara, Urban Outfitters, Bougessa, and EDT Amsterdam. The former 2 websites are large-scale fashion powerhouses, managing thousands of products and making a large amount of revenue online each day. The latter are small-scale websites, only managing around a hundred products at a time, but still have very professionally designed websites. Looking at these, I gave suggestions, looked at what they did well, and eventually formed a criteria that would be used when making my own website.

I then came to designing and developing the website, using all of the knowledge I had acquired during the previous stages. I primarily used PHP and SQL to handle the back-end, as well as creating a custom CSS stylesheet, with elements of Jquery to add dynamic elements such as a pop-up cart and photo gallery.The end result was an E-Commerce website that was professional, easy to use, and had an emphasis on helping the user in their journey.
